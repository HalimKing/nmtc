Management Pays Courtesy Visit to Chief to Foster Institutional Development

NMTC Communications

Public Relations Office

Share:

Management team of NMTC Tamale during the courtesy visit to the traditional authority

TAMALE, Northern Region - In a significant move to strengthen community partnerships and foster institutional development, the management team of the Nurses and Midwives Training College, Tamale, has paid a courtesy call on His Royal Highness, the revered traditional chief of the area.

The delegation, led by the College Principal, Mrs. Sarah Mensah, and accompanied by senior administrative staff, was warmly received at the chief's palace on Thursday, December 15, 2024. The meeting was aimed at discussing collaborative opportunities that could benefit both the institution and the broader community.

Building Stronger Community Ties

During the visit, Mrs. Mensah emphasized the college's commitment to working closely with traditional authorities to ensure that the institution remains deeply rooted in community values while maintaining its academic excellence. "Our students are not just future healthcare professionals; they are members of this community who will serve both locally and nationally," she stated.

"The college has always been a beacon of hope for our community, and we are proud to support its continued growth and development. Our doors are always open for collaboration that benefits our people." - His Royal Highness, Traditional Chief

The chief, in his response, commended the college management for their proactive approach to community engagement. He highlighted the significant impact that NMTC Tamale graduates have had on healthcare delivery in the region over the years.

Areas of Collaboration Discussed

The meeting covered several key areas where the college and traditional authority could work together:

  • Community Health Outreach Programs: Expanding student-led health education and screening programs in rural communities
  • Cultural Integration: Incorporating local cultural practices and traditional healing knowledge into the nursing curriculum
  • Infrastructure Development: Seeking community support for ongoing expansion projects at the college
  • Student Accommodation: Exploring opportunities for safe, affordable housing options for students
  • Employment Opportunities: Creating pathways for graduates to serve in community health centers and clinics

Future Plans and Commitments

The meeting concluded with both parties expressing their commitment to regular dialogue and collaboration. Plans were made for the chief to visit the college campus in the coming month to see firsthand the facilities and ongoing programs.

Additionally, the college management extended an invitation to the traditional authority to participate in the upcoming graduation ceremony, where over 150 students are expected to receive their diplomas in various nursing and midwifery programs.

Impact on Students and Community

This initiative is expected to have far-reaching positive effects on both current students and the wider community. Students will benefit from enhanced practical learning opportunities through community engagement, while local residents will have improved access to health education and basic medical services.

The college has already seen increased community support since the announcement of this partnership, with several local organizations expressing interest in supporting student programs and activities.

As NMTC Tamale continues to grow and evolve, partnerships like this one demonstrate the institution's commitment to being not just an educational center, but a true community partner dedicated to improving healthcare outcomes across the Northern Region.

For more information about NMTC Tamale's community programs and partnerships, visit our website or contact the Public Relations Office at communications@nmtctamale.edu.gh
